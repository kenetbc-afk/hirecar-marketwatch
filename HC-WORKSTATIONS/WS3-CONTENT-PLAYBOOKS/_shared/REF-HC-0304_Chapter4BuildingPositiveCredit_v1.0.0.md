## THEME — From Defense to Offense

The first three chapters of this playbook were about defense — understanding your report, disputing errors, handling collections. That work was necessary, and every item you removed or resolved is already showing up (or will soon show up) as points back on your score. But defense only takes you so far. To get where you want to go — better vehicle loans, lower insurance rates, business funding, fleet expansion — you need to actively build positive credit history.

Think of it this way: removing negatives stops the bleeding, but adding positives is what builds the muscle. Lenders do not just look at what went wrong — they look at what is going right. They want to see active accounts in good standing, consistent on-time payments, and a healthy mix of credit types. They want evidence that you can manage money responsibly over time.

For rideshare operators, this is where the game changes. The credit system was not designed with gig workers in mind. You do not have a W-2 that proves steady income. You do not have an employer who vouches for your reliability. What you have is your credit profile — and it needs to tell a strong story.

The operators who graduate from HIRECAR's credit program and move into fleet ownership, business funding, and scaling — they all share one thing in common. They did not just clean up their reports. They deliberately built a credit profile that opens doors. Authorized user tradelines, secured credit cards, credit builder loans, and — the biggest unlock of all — business credit separation through an EIN.

This chapter gives you the HIRECAR Credit Building Stack: five strategies you can start implementing today, each one adding points to your score and strength to your financial profile. Combined with the defense work from Chapters 1 through 3, this positions you for the funding and financing that most rideshare operators think is out of reach.

Every action you take from here is an investment. A secured card used responsibly for three months can add 20 to 30 points. An authorized user tradeline can add 15 to 45 points in 30 to 60 days. Separating your business and personal credit opens an entirely new world of funding options. The math is real, the timeline is achievable, and the tools are in your hands.

## STRATEGY — The HIRECAR Credit Building Stack

The Credit Building Stack is a 5-layer strategy. Each layer adds to the one before it. Work through them in order for maximum impact.

### Layer 1: Authorized User Tradelines

An authorized user tradeline is when someone adds you to their existing credit card account. You do not need to use the card or even receive one — the account's positive history appears on your credit report. This is one of the fastest ways to add positive history to your profile.

**Best for:** Members with thin credit files (few accounts) or those who need a quick score boost while other strategies build over time.

**Potential impact:** 15 to 45 points within 1 to 2 billing cycles, depending on the age and limit of the account.

**Requirements:** You need someone with good credit who trusts you enough to add you — a family member, partner, or close friend. The account should have a long history, low utilization (under 30%), and zero late payments.

### Layer 2: Secured Credit Cards

A secured credit card requires a cash deposit (typically $200 to $500) that becomes your credit limit. You use the card for small purchases and pay the balance in full every month. After 6 to 12 months of responsible use, most issuers will upgrade you to an unsecured card and return your deposit.

**Best for:** Members rebuilding credit who need to establish a pattern of on-time payments.

**Potential impact:** 20 to 30 points over 3 to 6 months of consistent on-time payments.

**Key rules:** Never use more than 30% of your limit. Pay the full balance every month, not just the minimum. Set up autopay to avoid missed payments. Choose a card that reports to all 3 bureaus.

### Layer 3: Credit Builder Loans

A credit builder loan is designed specifically to build credit. Instead of receiving money upfront, the lender holds the loan amount in a savings account. You make monthly payments for 6 to 24 months. Once the loan is paid off, you receive the money plus any interest earned. Every on-time payment is reported to the bureaus as positive history.

**Best for:** Members who want to add an installment loan to their credit mix (diversifying beyond just credit cards).

**Potential impact:** 15 to 25 points over 6 to 12 months, plus you build a savings cushion.

**Tip:** Some credit unions and fintech lenders offer credit builder loans with no hard credit check to apply.

### Layer 4: Business Credit Separation (EIN vs SSN)

This is the most powerful long-term strategy for rideshare operators. By establishing business credit under your EIN (Employer Identification Number) instead of your SSN, you create a separate credit profile for your rideshare business.

**Why this matters:** Business credit cards, business lines of credit, and fleet financing can be obtained based on your business credit profile — not your personal credit. This means your personal credit score does not need to be perfect to access business funding.

**Steps to separate:**
1. Register your rideshare business as an LLC (or verify your existing registration)
2. Get an EIN from the IRS (free, takes 5 minutes online at irs.gov)
3. Open a business bank account under your EIN
4. Get a DUNS Number from Dun & Bradstreet (free registration)
5. Start with vendor credit accounts that report to business credit bureaus (Dun & Bradstreet, Experian Business, Equifax Business)
6. Build up to business credit cards and lines of credit

## APPROACH — Step-by-Step Using HIRECAR Tools

### How to Find an Authorized User Tradeline

1. Identify potential candidates: family members, partners, or close friends with strong credit
2. Explain what you are asking: They add you as an authorized user to one of their credit cards. They do not need to give you the card.
3. Choose the right account: Look for cards with at least 3 to 5 years of history, a high credit limit, zero late payments, and low utilization (under 30%)
4. Have them contact their card issuer to add you — they will need your name and SSN
5. Wait 1 to 2 billing cycles for the tradeline to appear on your credit report
6. Monitor: check your credit report to confirm the tradeline has been added

**Caution:** If the primary cardholder misses a payment or maxes out the card, it negatively affects your credit too. Only partner with someone whose financial habits you trust.

### How to Choose a Secured Credit Card

When evaluating secured credit cards, look for:
- **Reports to all 3 bureaus** — This is non-negotiable. If a card only reports to one bureau, it does half the work.
- **Upgrade path** — Choose a card that will upgrade to an unsecured card after 6 to 12 months of good standing
- **Low or no annual fee** — You are building credit, not paying for the privilege
- **No application fee** — Some predatory cards charge fees just to apply

Use your secured card for one or two small recurring purchases (streaming subscription, phone bill) and pay the full balance every month. This creates a pattern of consistent, on-time payments without the temptation to overspend.

### How to Set Up a Credit Builder Loan

1. Research credit builder loan providers — credit unions, community development financial institutions (CDFIs), and fintech lenders like Self or MoneyLion
2. Choose a loan amount and term: $25 to $100 per month for 12 to 24 months is typical
3. Apply — many providers do not require a hard credit check
4. Set up automatic payments — one missed payment defeats the purpose
5. Continue until the loan term ends — you receive the saved funds as a lump sum
6. Log the loan in your Credit Tracker as an active positive tradeline

### How to Start Building Business Credit

**Month 1: Establish Your Business Identity**
- Register your LLC (if not already done) through your state's Secretary of State
- Apply for an EIN at irs.gov/ein (free, instant online)
- Open a business checking account at a local bank or credit union

**Month 2: Get Your DUNS Number**
- Register at dnb.com for a free DUNS Number
- This is your business credit identity — equivalent to your SSN for personal credit

**Month 3-6: Open Vendor Credit Accounts**
- Start with vendors that report to business credit bureaus and have easy approval requirements
- Categories to explore: office supplies, fuel cards, fleet maintenance accounts
- Use each account, pay on time (or early), and let the positive history build

**Month 6+: Apply for Business Credit Cards**
- Once you have 3 to 5 reporting vendor accounts with on-time payment history, you become eligible for business credit cards
- Business credit cards often have higher limits than personal cards and do not always report to personal credit bureaus
