# Chapter 3: Collections & Protection

*Defense is part of the game, {{member_name}}. When creditors come calling, you need a playbook — not panic.*

---

## THEME — Collections Are a Solvable Problem

If you have ever had your phone ring at 7 AM from a number you did not recognize, only to hear someone demanding money for an account you barely remember — you already know how it feels to be on the wrong end of collections. For rideshare operators, this is painfully common. Income fluctuates. A slow month can mean a missed payment. A medical emergency during a period without health coverage can send a $3,000 bill straight to a collection agency before you even open the first letter.

Here is what the collection industry does not want you to know: **you have more power than they do.** The Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA) is a federal law that sets strict rules on what collectors can and cannot do. They cannot call you before 8 AM or after 9 PM. They cannot threaten you with actions they have no intention of taking. They cannot discuss your debt with your family, your employer, or anyone other than you. And most importantly — under **FDCPA Section 809** — they must validate the debt if you ask them to.

Debt validation means the collector has to prove three things: (1) the debt is yours, (2) the amount is correct, and (3) they are legally authorized to collect it. If they cannot prove all three, they cannot legally continue collection activity. This one right — the right to demand validation — stops more illegitimate collections than any other tool in consumer protection.

But defense alone is not enough. HIRECAR's Collections Defense goes beyond just stopping the calls. It gives you a framework to evaluate each collection account strategically: Which ones are worth negotiating? Which ones can be disputed off your report entirely? Which ones are time-barred debts that no longer have legal teeth?

You have already identified the negative items on your report in Chapter 1 and started disputing inaccuracies in Chapter 2. Now it is time to deal with the collections — the accounts that have been sold to third-party agencies, the debts that have been sitting on your report silently dragging your score down. This chapter teaches you how to fight the ones that should not be there and negotiate the ones that are legitimate.

## STRATEGY — HIRECAR Collections Defense

The Collections Defense framework has four pillars. Work through them in order for each collection account on your report.

### Pillar 1: Validate Every Debt

Before you pay anything, before you negotiate anything — send a **Debt Validation Letter** (Template 3 from your HIRECAR Templates). Under FDCPA §809, you have 30 days from the first contact by a collector to request validation. Even if more than 30 days have passed, many collectors will still respond to validation requests because they want your money.

Your validation letter requests:
- Proof that the debt is yours (original signed agreement or application)
- Verification of the exact amount owed, including all fees and interest
- Proof that the collection agency is licensed to collect in your state
- A complete payment history from the original creditor

If the collector cannot validate the debt, they must cease collection activity and remove the item from your credit report.

### Pillar 2: Know the Statute of Limitations

Every debt has a statute of limitations — a legal time limit on when a creditor can sue you for the balance. Once the statute expires, the debt is **time-barred**. The creditor can still ask you to pay, but they cannot take you to court.

**Critical rule: never restart the clock.** Making a payment, acknowledging the debt in writing, or even making a verbal promise to pay can restart the statute of limitations in some states. Before you engage with any collector, check the status.

Statutes of limitation vary by state and debt type. For California (the primary HIRECAR market): **4 years** on written contracts and credit card debt, **2 years** on oral agreements. Members in other states should verify their local statutes — the HIRECAR dashboard provides a reference lookup.

### Pillar 3: Negotiate from Strength

For debts that are validated and within the statute of limitations, you have three negotiation options:

**Pay-for-Delete:** You offer to pay the balance (or a portion) in exchange for the collector removing the item from your credit report entirely. Not all collectors accept this, but many will — especially on older debts. Always get the agreement in writing before you pay.

**Settlement:** You negotiate a lower payoff amount — typically 30% to 60% of the original balance. Settled accounts may still show on your report as "settled for less than full amount," which is better than an open collection but not as clean as a deletion.

**Full Payment + Goodwill Request:** If you can pay the full amount, pay it and then send a Goodwill Deletion Request (Template 5) asking the creditor to remove the negative mark as a courtesy. This works best when you have a strong payment history with the creditor outside of the collection.

### Pillar 4: Never Restart the Clock on Time-Barred Debts

If a debt is past the statute of limitations:
- Do not make a payment, even a small one
- Do not acknowledge the debt in writing
- Do not make any verbal promises to pay
- You can send a Cease and Desist letter (Template 4) to stop all communication

Time-barred debts will eventually fall off your credit report after **7 years** from the date of first delinquency. If a collector is reporting a time-barred debt as new activity, dispute it immediately — this is a violation of the FCRA.

## APPROACH — Step-by-Step Using HIRECAR Tools

### How to Send a Debt Validation Letter

1. Open Template 3 (Debt Validation Letter) from your HIRECAR Templates
2. Fill in the bracketed fields:
   - [Your Name and Address]
   - [Collection Agency Name and Address] — found on your credit report or in collection notices you have received
   - [Alleged Amount Owed]
   - [Original Creditor Name]
   - [Account or Reference Number]
3. Print the letter and make a copy for your records
4. Send via **certified mail with return receipt requested** — this creates legal proof of delivery
5. Log the letter in your Collections Tracker:

| Field | What to Record |
|---|---|
| Collection Agency | Name of the collection agency |
| Original Creditor | Who the debt was originally owed to |
| Amount Claimed | Dollar amount the collector claims |
| Date Sent | When you mailed the validation letter |
| Tracking Number | USPS certified mail tracking |
| Response Deadline | 30 days from delivery |
| Status | Sent / Validated / Failed Validation / Resolved |
| Outcome | Removed / Settled / Paid / Disputed |

### How to Check the Statute of Limitations

1. Identify the debt type: credit card, medical, personal loan, auto loan
2. Identify the state where the debt was incurred (not where you live now, but where the original agreement was signed)
3. Look up the statute of limitations for that debt type in that state
4. Calculate: Date of Last Payment + Statute Years = Expiration Date
5. If the expiration date has passed, the debt is time-barred — do not make a payment

### How to Negotiate: Phone Script for Collections Calls

If a collector calls and the debt is validated and within the statute:

**Opening:** "I am calling regarding account [number]. I am prepared to discuss resolution but I need to understand my options."

**If they demand full payment:** "I understand the full amount is [X]. I am willing to resolve this today for [offer 30-50% of the balance]. Can you authorize a settlement at that amount with deletion from my credit report?"

**If they agree:** "I need that agreement in writing before I make any payment. Please send a settlement letter confirming the amount, the payment deadline, and that the account will be deleted from all three credit bureaus upon receipt of payment."

**If they refuse deletion:** "I understand. In that case, I need the settlement letter to confirm the account will be reported as Paid in Full, not Settled for Less Than, upon receipt of payment."

**Always:** Get everything in writing. Never give bank account or debit card numbers over the phone — use a money order or cashier's check.

### How to Use the Cease and Desist Letter

For debts that are time-barred or for collectors who are harassing you:

1. Open Template 4 (Cease & Desist) from your HIRECAR Templates
2. Fill in the agency name, your account reference, and your details
3. Send via certified mail
4. Under FDCPA §805(c), the collector may only contact you after receiving this letter to: (a) confirm they will stop contacting you, or (b) notify you of a specific legal action they intend to take
5. If they continue calling after receiving your Cease & Desist, this is a FDCPA violation — document every call with date, time, and what was said
