## THEME — Your Right to Accurate Reporting

Here is something most people never learn: you have a federally protected right to accurate credit reporting. It is not a suggestion. It is the law. The Fair Credit Reporting Act — specifically **Section 611** — gives you the power to dispute any information on your credit report that you believe is inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable. When you file a dispute, the credit bureau is legally required to investigate within 30 days. If they cannot verify the item, they must remove it.

This is not a loophole. This is consumer protection working exactly as designed.

For rideshare operators, this matters more than most people realize. That collection account from two years ago — the one you already paid but that still shows as open? It could be costing you 50 to 100 points on your score. That late payment from a billing error during a slow month? It is sitting there quietly making lenders charge you more for every vehicle loan. These are not permanent marks. They are data points, and data points can be corrected.

The credit bureaus process millions of disputes every year. They are required to give your dispute a fair investigation under the law. And here is the part that changes everything: under **FCRA Section 623**, the companies that report information to the bureaus — your creditors, collection agencies, lenders — also have legal obligations. If they reported something wrong, they are required to correct it. If they cannot prove the debt is yours, the item must come off.

HIRECAR operators who take dispute strategy seriously see results. Not because they are gaming the system, but because they are exercising their legal rights to ensure their credit reports are accurate. Every error removed is a score increase. Every inaccuracy corrected is a better loan rate, a cheaper insurance premium, and one step closer to the business funding that will take you from driving to owning.

You did the hard work in Chapter 1 — pulling your reports and identifying the negatives. Now you have a clear target list. This chapter gives you the strategy and tools to go after every item that does not belong on your report.

## STRATEGY — The HIRECAR Dispute Framework

HIRECAR uses a 5-step dispute framework. Each step builds on the last. Follow them in order.

### Step 1: Identify the Dispute Angle

Every negative item on your report falls into one of five dispute categories. The angle you choose determines which template letter you use and how you frame your dispute:

- **Inaccuracy** — The reported information is factually wrong: wrong balance, wrong date, wrong payment status, wrong account number
- **Identity** — The account does not belong to you, or your identity information has been mixed with someone else's file
- **Obsolete** — The item has exceeded its reporting time limit (most negative items must be removed after 7 years; bankruptcies after 10 years)
- **Unverifiable** — The creditor or bureau cannot produce documentation proving the item is accurate and belongs to you
- **Negotiable** — The item is technically accurate but may be removable through goodwill requests or pay-for-delete negotiations

### Step 2: Select the Right Letter Template

HIRECAR provides 5 dispute letter templates (available in your Templates section). Match the template to your dispute angle:

| Dispute Angle | Template | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Inaccuracy | Template 1: Inaccuracy Dispute | *FCRA §611* |
| Identity | Template 2: Identity Theft Dispute | *FCRA §605B* |
| Debt Validation | Template 3: Debt Validation Letter | *FDCPA §809* |
| Cease Communication | Template 4: Cease & Desist | *FDCPA §805(c)* |
| Negotiable | Template 5: Goodwill Deletion Request | Courtesy request |

### Step 3: Submit via Certified Mail with Tracking

Always send dispute letters by **certified mail with return receipt requested** through USPS. This creates a legal record that the bureau or creditor received your letter. Online dispute portals are convenient, but certified mail gives you stronger documentation if you need to escalate. Keep copies of everything you send.

### Step 4: Track the 30-Day Response Window

Under FCRA, credit bureaus must complete their investigation within **30 days** of receiving your dispute (45 days if you submit additional information during the investigation). Mark the date on your calendar. If the 30-day window passes without a response, the bureau is required to remove the disputed item.

### Step 5: Follow Up or Escalate

After the investigation, the bureau will send you results. Three possible outcomes:

- **Item removed or corrected** — Victory. Verify on your next report pull.
- **Item verified as accurate** — You can escalate: file with a different bureau, submit additional evidence, or file a complaint with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB).
- **No response within 30 days** — Send a follow-up letter demanding removal per FCRA requirements.

## APPROACH — Step-by-Step Using HIRECAR Tools

### Selecting Items from Your Credit Report Log

Open the Credit Tracker you built in Chapter 1. Sort your negative items by **disputability level**:

1. Start with **High disputability** items — these have the strongest chance of removal
2. Move to **Medium disputability** — these may require negotiation
3. Save **Low disputability** items for time-based strategies in Chapter 4

For each item you are ready to dispute, note the bureau it appears on. If the same item appears on multiple bureaus, you will need to send separate letters to each bureau.

### Choosing and Customizing Your Dispute Letter

Navigate to your HIRECAR Templates section. Select the template that matches your dispute angle (see the table in Step 2 above). Each template includes bracketed fields that you fill in with your specific information:

**For Template 1 (Inaccuracy Dispute):**
- [Your Name], [Your Address], [SSN last 4 digits]
- [Bureau Name and Address]
- [Account Number], [Creditor Name]
- [Specific Inaccuracy] — describe exactly what is wrong
- [Requested Correction] — state what the report should say instead

**For Template 3 (Debt Validation):**
- [Collection Agency Name and Address]
- [Alleged Amount Owed]
- [Original Creditor Name]
- Request validation of the debt, proof of authorization to collect, and a complete payment history

Customize each letter with the specific details from your Credit Tracker. Generic letters get generic results — the more specific you are about the inaccuracy, the stronger your dispute.

### Submitting Your Dispute

**Certified Mail Method (Recommended):**
1. Print your completed dispute letter
2. Attach copies (never originals) of any supporting documents: payment receipts, account statements, identity documents
3. Take the letter to USPS and request **Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested**
4. Keep the tracking number and receipt
5. The return receipt (green card) will come back to you as proof of delivery

**Online Dispute Portals (Alternative):**
- Equifax: equifax.com/personal/disputes
- Experian: experian.com/disputes
- TransUnion: transunion.com/disputes

Online portals are faster but provide less documentation. If you use them, screenshot every step and save confirmation numbers.

### Tracking Your Disputes

Update your Credit Tracker with a Dispute Tracking Table for each active dispute:

| Field | What to Record |
|---|---|
| Item Description | The negative item you disputed |
| Bureau | Which bureau received the dispute |
| Template Used | Which HIRECAR template (1-5) |
| Date Sent | Date you mailed or submitted the dispute |
| Tracking Number | USPS certified mail tracking number |
| Response Due | 30 days from delivery date |
| Status | Sent / Under Investigation / Resolved |
| Outcome | Removed / Corrected / Verified / No Response |

Check your tracking status weekly. Set calendar reminders for the 30-day response deadline on each dispute.
