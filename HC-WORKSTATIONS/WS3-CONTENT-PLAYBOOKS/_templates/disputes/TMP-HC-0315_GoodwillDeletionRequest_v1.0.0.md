## Sending Instructions

1. **Print** this letter after filling in all bracketed fields — personalize it genuinely
2. **Do NOT use a threatening or demanding tone** — this is a courtesy request. Being polite and honest gives you the best chance.
3. **Send via regular mail** to the creditor's customer service or credit reporting department (certified mail is optional for goodwill requests since this is not a legal demand)
4. **Keep** a copy for your records
5. **Log** in your HIRECAR Dispute Tracking Table:
   - Creditor: [name]
   - Type: Goodwill Request
   - Date Sent: [date]
   - Status: Pending Response
6. **Follow up** — if you do not hear back in 30 days, call the creditor's customer service line and reference your letter

**Tips for Success:**
- **Be genuine** — Cookie-cutter letters are ignored. The more personal and specific your explanation, the better.
- **One item at a time** — Do not ask for multiple removals in one letter. Focus on the most impactful item.
- **Highlight your loyalty** — If you have a long history with the creditor or hold multiple accounts, mention it.
- **Be patient** — Some creditors respond within 2 weeks, others take 60 days. Some never respond. If denied, you can try again in 6 months or call instead of writing.
- **Phone alternative** — Some members have success calling the creditor's customer service line and asking verbally. If the representative agrees, ask them to send written confirmation before ending the call.

**Success rates vary.** Goodwill deletions are not guaranteed — they are entirely at the creditor's discretion. Factors that improve your chances: long customer history, single isolated incident (not a pattern), account now in good standing, and a sincere, well-written letter.
