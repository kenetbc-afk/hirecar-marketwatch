**RE: Identity Theft Dispute — Request for Blocking Under FCRA §605B**

To Whom It May Concern:

I am a victim of identity theft. I am writing to dispute fraudulent information appearing on my credit report that resulted from identity theft. Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act, Section 605B (15 U.S.C. §1681c-2), I am requesting that your bureau block the reporting of any information that resulted from the alleged identity theft.

I have enclosed copies of the required documentation as specified under FCRA §605B(a):

**Requested Actions:**

1. Block the fraudulent account(s) listed above from appearing on my credit report per FCRA §605B
2. Remove all inquiries associated with the fraudulent account(s)
3. Correct any personal information that was altered as a result of the identity theft (addresses, phone numbers, employers)
4. Send me a copy of my updated credit report reflecting these corrections
5. Place a fraud alert on my credit file if one is not already active

**Legal Notice:**

Under FCRA §605B(a), you are required to block the reporting of any information identified as resulting from identity theft within 4 business days of receiving this dispute, the identity theft report, the identification documentation, and this statement identifying the fraudulent information.

Under FCRA §605B(b), you must promptly notify the furnisher of information that the information has been blocked and may be the result of identity theft.

I certify that the representations made in this letter are true and correct. I understand that knowingly making false representations to a consumer reporting agency regarding identity theft is a federal crime under 18 U.S.C. §1028.

## Sending Instructions

1. **Print** this letter after filling in all bracketed fields and checking all applicable documentation boxes
2. **Attach copies** of: government ID, FTC Identity Theft Report, police report, and any other supporting evidence
3. **Send via USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested**
4. **Also send copies** to the creditor(s) associated with the fraudulent accounts
5. **Log** in your HIRECAR Dispute Tracking Table with Identity Theft designation
6. **Set a calendar reminder** — bureaus must block fraudulent items within **4 business days** of receiving complete documentation

**Additional Steps for Identity Theft Victims:**
- File a report at **identitytheft.gov** (FTC) if you have not already
- File a police report with your local department
- Place a **fraud alert** or **credit freeze** on all 3 bureaus
- Consider placing an **extended fraud alert** (lasts 7 years) for ongoing protection
