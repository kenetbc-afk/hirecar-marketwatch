**Account/Reference Number:** [ACCOUNT OR REFERENCE NUMBER]
**Alleged Amount:** [DOLLAR AMOUNT CLAIMED, if known]

To Whom It May Concern:

I am writing to formally demand that you cease all communication with me regarding the above-referenced account, effective immediately. This demand is made pursuant to my rights under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act, Section 805(c) (15 U.S.C. §1692c(c)).

**I demand that you immediately cease:**

- All phone calls to me, my family, my employer, and any third parties
- All letters, emails, text messages, and any other written communication
- All contact through any medium regarding this alleged debt

**Legal Notice:**

Under FDCPA §805(c), once you receive this written notice, you may not communicate with me further except to:

1. Advise me that your collection efforts are being terminated
2. Notify me that you or the creditor may invoke a specific legal remedy (such as filing a lawsuit)
3. Notify me that you or the creditor intend to invoke a specific legal remedy

Any communication beyond these three exceptions after your receipt of this letter will constitute a violation of the FDCPA. I am aware of my rights under the FDCPA and will pursue all available remedies for any violations, including statutory damages under §813 (15 U.S.C. §1692k).

**Additional Demands:**

- If you are reporting this account to any credit bureau, I dispute the accuracy of this reporting and request that you investigate and correct or remove any inaccurate information per FCRA §623
- Do not sell, assign, or transfer this account to another collection agency without disclosing the existence of this cease communication notice

**Documentation:**

I am sending this letter via USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested. The return receipt will serve as proof that you received this demand. I am retaining a copy of this letter and the certified mail receipt for my records.

This letter is not an acknowledgment of any debt, nor is it a promise to pay. I reserve all rights available to me under federal and state law.

## Sending Instructions

1. **Print** this letter after filling in all bracketed fields
2. **Do NOT include** any payment information, bank details, or SSN
3. **Send via USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested**
4. **Keep** the certified mail receipt, tracking number, return receipt (green card), and a copy of the letter
5. **Log** in your HIRECAR Collections Tracker:
   - Collection Agency: [name]
   - Action: Cease & Desist Sent
   - Date Sent: [date]
   - Tracking: [USPS number]
   - Status: Communication Ceased
6. **Document any future contact** — if the collector contacts you after receiving this letter (beyond the 3 permitted exceptions), record the date, time, method of contact, and what was said

**When to Use This Template:**
- The debt is **time-barred** (past the statute of limitations) and you want the collector to stop contacting you
- You have already sent a **Debt Validation Letter** and the collector failed to validate but continues to contact you
- The collector is engaging in **harassment** — calling excessively, using threatening language, contacting your workplace or family members
- You have decided you do not wish to engage with the collector further for any reason

**Important:** Sending a Cease & Desist does not make the debt go away and does not remove it from your credit report. It only stops the collector from contacting you. The creditor may still choose to file a lawsuit (for debts within the statute of limitations). If you receive legal papers, do not ignore them — consult a consumer rights attorney.
