**RE: Demand for Validation of Alleged Debt — FDCPA §809(b)**

**Account/Reference Number:** [ACCOUNT OR REFERENCE NUMBER]
**Alleged Amount:** [DOLLAR AMOUNT CLAIMED]

To Whom It May Concern:

I am writing in response to [your letter dated [DATE] / your phone call on [DATE] / an account appearing on my credit report] regarding the above-referenced alleged debt. I am exercising my rights under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act, Section 809(b) (15 U.S.C. §1692g(b)), to request validation of this debt.

**I am formally requesting the following documentation:**

1. **Proof of the debt:** A copy of the original signed agreement, contract, or application bearing my signature that created this alleged obligation

2. **Verification of the amount:** A complete and itemized accounting of the total amount claimed, including:
   - Original principal balance
   - All interest charges applied
   - All fees assessed (late fees, collection fees, legal fees, etc.)
   - All payments received and credited
   - Current balance claimed

3. **Proof of authorization to collect:** Documentation showing that your agency is licensed to collect debts in [YOUR STATE] and that you have been authorized by the original creditor to collect this specific debt, including:
   - Copy of the assignment or purchase agreement between the original creditor and your agency
   - Chain of title if the debt has been sold

4. **Original creditor information:** The name and address of the original creditor, the original account number, and the date of the alleged original default

5. **Complete payment history:** A full payment history from the original creditor showing all transactions on the account from opening to default

**Important Notices:**

Until you provide adequate validation of this debt, I am requesting that you:

- **Cease all collection activity** on this account, including phone calls, letters, and any communication with third parties
- **Remove this account** from my credit report with all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion) if you are unable to provide the requested validation
- **Do not sell or transfer** this account to another entity until validation has been provided

**Legal Notice:**

Under FDCPA §809(b), upon receipt of this written request for validation within 30 days of your initial communication, you must cease collection of the debt until you have mailed verification of the debt to me.

Under FDCPA §807 (15 U.S.C. §1692e), it is a violation of federal law to continue to report unverified information to credit bureaus. Continued collection activity or credit reporting without providing the requested validation may constitute a violation of the FDCPA and FCRA.

I am not refusing to pay a legitimate debt. I am exercising my legal right to require proper verification before making any payment. If you can provide the documentation requested above, I am willing to discuss resolution of this matter.

Please respond to this request in writing to the address listed above.

## Sending Instructions

1. **Print** this letter after filling in all bracketed fields
2. **Do NOT include** any payment, bank account numbers, or SSN in this letter
3. **Send via USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested** to the collection agency address
4. **Keep** your certified mail receipt, tracking number, and a copy of the letter
5. **Log** in your HIRECAR Collections Tracker:
   - Collection Agency: [name]
   - Amount Claimed: [amount]
   - Date Sent: [date]
   - Tracking: [USPS number]
   - Status: Validation Requested
6. **Set a calendar reminder** — the collector must cease activity until validation is provided

**What to Expect:**
- If the collector **provides validation**: Review the documentation carefully. If legitimate, proceed to negotiation (see Chapter 3: Pay-for-Delete or Settlement strategies).
- If the collector **cannot validate**: They must cease collection and remove the item from your credit report. If they do not, file a complaint with the CFPB and your state attorney general.
- If the collector **ignores your request** and continues collection: This is a FDCPA violation. Document everything and consider consulting a consumer rights attorney.
