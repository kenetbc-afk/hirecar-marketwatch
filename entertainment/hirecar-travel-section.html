How Groupon Gets Deals
The Groupon Model
Groupon acts as a merchant of record — they negotiate directly with hotels, resorts, and experience operators to offer inventory at discounted rates, typically 30–70% off, in exchange for volume and upfront payment. They use three primary deal acquisition channels:

1. Direct Merchant Agreements
Hotels and resorts fill unsold inventory (especially midweek / off-season) through Groupon rather than let rooms go empty. Groupon takes a 15–25% commission. The hotel gets cash flow; Groupon gets the margin.
2. OTA API Feeds (Booking.com, Expedia)
Much of Groupon's travel inventory is pulled from OTA feeds. They present the same inventory with FOMO framing ("was $389, now $214") and earn referral/affiliate commissions.
3. Package Tour Operators
Companies like "Great Value Vacations" and "Weekender Breaks" package air + hotel and wholesale to Groupon. Groupon marks them up and adds urgency.
How HIRECAR Can Replicate This
The HIRECAR Deal Engine
HIRECAR has a structural advantage Groupon doesn't — a captive audience of premium vehicle users who are already traveling. Here's the playbook:

Phase 1: OTA Affiliate Partnerships
Sign up for Hotels.com, Expedia, and Booking.com affiliate programs. Pull live deals into this section via their APIs. Zero inventory risk — earn 4–8% commission on every booking. Low code lift to start.
Phase 2: HIRECAR Bundle Packages
Negotiate directly with 3–5 resorts near LA (Palm Springs, Big Bear, Temecula) to offer exclusive "HIRECAR Package" rates: Hotel + First Class Vehicle = one bundle price. HIRECAR controls pricing and earns on both sides.
Phase 3: First Class Member Exclusives
Gate the best deals (casino packages, golf resorts, all-inclusive) behind the $1,380/yr membership. This drives membership conversion while the deals section pays for itself via affiliate + bundle revenue.
Phase 4: Co-op Marketing with Casinos
MGM, Pechanga, Agua Caliente, and Primm Valley all have affiliate / referral programs. Negotiate group transport contracts with their VIP departments — casinos often comp room nights for operators who drive volume.
