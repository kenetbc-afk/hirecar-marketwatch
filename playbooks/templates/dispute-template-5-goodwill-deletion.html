COURTESY REQUEST - NOT A LEGAL DEMAND This is a goodwill request for account deletion. It is NOT based on legal requirements but rather on the creditor's discretion. Success rates vary, but it's worth trying before pursuing legal disputes.

Note: This is a courtesy request, not a legal demand. Credit reporting practices are at the creditor's discretion, but many creditors are willing to make goodwill adjustments for valued customers with otherwise positive payment history.

How to Send This Letter
1. Print this letter on your letterhead or plain white paper.
2. Sign the letter by hand above your typed name.
3. Make 2-3 copies of the complete letter.
4. Gather supporting documentation: recent statements showing current payment status, proof of payment, letters from creditor, or any correspondence showing the account is in good standing.
5. Send via Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested. While this is a courtesy request, certified mail provides proof of your communication.
6. Keep copies of all documents and the certified mail receipt.
7. IMPORTANT: You can also call the creditor's customer service line and ask to speak with a supervisor or manager. Sometimes verbal requests combined with written follow-up are more effective.
8. Document all contact attempts, responses, and outcomes.
Expected Timeline & Next Steps
Day 1:
Mail certified letter or call creditor supervisor
Day 3-7:
Creditor receives mail (check return receipt)
Day 7-30:
Creditor may call or respond in writing with decision
Day 30-60:
Follow up if no response; request decision in writing
Day 60+:
If denied, consider legal dispute letter (Template 1) or debt validation letter (Template 3)
Success Tips: Goodwill deletions are more likely if: (1) the delinquency is recent or isolated, (2) you have a long history of on-time payments before the incident, (3) the account is now in good standing, and (4) you approach the creditor respectfully. If denied, you have legal options through formal dispute processes.
